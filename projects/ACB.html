Automated ACB Re-powering System Using PLC

Project Overview

The Automated ACB Re-powering System is an industrial automation solution developed for Colombo International Container Terminals (CICT) to restore electrical power automatically after power disturbances. The system uses a PLC-controlled motorized mechanism to reclose Air Circuit Breakers (ACBs) without requiring manual intervention, significantly reducing recovery time and improving operational continuity within the E-RTG container yard.

Problem Statement

CICT's E-RTG yard receives power through a DC 680V bus bar system and multiple Masterpact NT Air Circuit Breakers. During power sags or outages, converter stations trip and all ACBs open as a protective measure. Restoring power requires technicians to manually reclose each breaker, which can result in lengthy operational interruptions and reduced terminal productivity. The objective of this project was to automate the re-powering process and minimize downtime following electrical disturbances.

System Architecture

  • Power interruption causes the converter station to trip.
  • ACBs open automatically to protect the electrical system.
  • PLC monitors system status and initiates recovery logic.
  • A motorized actuation mechanism performs breaker reclosing.
  • Control logic verifies safe operating conditions.
  • Power is restored automatically with minimal human intervention.
  • System status can be monitored through the control panel.

Results & Impact

The solution improves terminal productivity, increases equipment availability, and enhances the reliability of electrical power restoration within the E-RTG yard. The project demonstrates the effective application of PLC-based automation in critical industrial power systems.
